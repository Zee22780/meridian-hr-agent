# Your First Week at Meridian

**Last Updated:** January 2026  
**For:** New Hires

---

## Welcome

Starting a new job comes with a lot of questions. This guide walks you through what to expect during your first week at Meridian — who you'll meet, what you'll need to set up, and what you don't need to worry about yet.

Your onboarding is handled by the Meridian HR Agent and supported by your HR rep, Nadia Osei (nadia.osei@meridian.com). If anything feels unclear or missing, reach out to her directly.

---

## Before Day One

If you haven't already, make sure the following are complete before your start date:

- **Direct deposit form** — submitted through the HR portal (required for on-time first paycheck)
- **W-4** — tax withholding elections, also in the HR portal
- **Emergency contact information** — in the HR portal under your employee profile
- **Equipment delivery** — if you're remote, confirm your shipping address is correct in the portal; expect your laptop to arrive 1–2 business days before your start date

You'll receive a welcome email from the Meridian HR Agent with your HR portal login credentials and a checklist of pre-start tasks. Complete these as early as possible — late submissions can delay your first paycheck or benefits enrollment.

---

## Day One

### Morning

Your first morning is intentionally light. You don't need to hit the ground running — you need to get set up.

- **IT setup meeting** — you'll have a 90-minute session with Alex Park (IT Support) to get your laptop configured, accounts provisioned, and tools installed. This happens first thing.
- **Manager check-in** — a 60-minute welcome meeting with your manager. Expect to talk about your role, your team, and what the first 30 days will look like. This is a conversation, not a performance review.
- **HR orientation** — a 60-minute session with Nadia Osei (HR) to walk through your benefits options, confirm your paperwork, and answer any questions. Benefits enrollment must be completed within 30 days of your start date.

### Afternoon

- Get familiar with your tools — Slack, Notion, Linear (if Engineering), Google Workspace
- Review the employee handbook sections most relevant to your role
- Say hello to your team on Slack — your manager will introduce you in your team channel

**Don't worry about:** deep work, shipping anything, or knowing everything. Day one is about setup and orientation.

---

## Day Two

- **HR rep follow-up** — Nadia may check in to confirm your paperwork is complete and answer any benefits questions from day one
- Begin working through your onboarding checklist in the HR portal (your manager will have added role-specific tasks)
- First 1:1 with your buddy — your peer contact will reach out to schedule time if they haven't already. This is informal — no agenda required.

---

## Days Three and Four

By mid-week, you should have your tools set up and a basic sense of your team's rhythm. This is when your broader onboarding meetings begin.

- **Department head meeting** — a 30–60 minute intro with your department head. Expect an overview of team goals, current priorities, and how your role fits in.
- **Marketing intro** — a 45-minute session with Diana Walsh (VP of Marketing). Every new hire at Meridian has this meeting regardless of department. Diana covers Meridian's brand story, market position, and how the company talks about itself externally.
- **Continue onboarding checklist** — role-specific tasks assigned by your manager

---

## End of Week One

### OG Employee Conversation

Sometime in your first two weeks (often scheduled toward the end of week one), you'll have a conversation with Tom Adler, Meridian's CEO and one of its original employees. This is one of Meridian's most valued onboarding traditions.

Tom's session is informal — no slides, no agenda. He talks about why Meridian was founded, what the early days looked like, and what the company stands for. Come with questions if you have them.

### Buddy Check-In

Your buddy will check in with you at the end of week one. Common topics:

- How is onboarding going?
- Any tools or processes that are confusing?
- Who else should you meet?
- What's the unwritten stuff you should know?

Your buddy is a peer, not your manager. You can be honest with them.

---

## Week Two

Week two is about going deeper. By now your tools are set up, you've met your core team, and you have a sense of the work.

- Continue working through your onboarding checklist
- Begin contributing to team work at whatever level your manager has outlined
- Complete any outstanding paperwork or benefits enrollment (deadline is 30 days from start)
- Begin scheduling any remaining onboarding meetings that haven't been confirmed yet

## What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

- **Equipment didn't arrive:** Contact Alex Park at it@meridian.com or message #it-support on Slack
- **Can't access a tool or system:** Same — IT is your first stop
- **Paycheck issue:** Contact people@meridian.com as soon as possible
- **Onboarding question not answered here:** Ask your HR rep, Nadia Osei, at nadia.osei@meridian.com
- **Something feels off with your team or manager:** You can speak confidentially with Nadia or use the anonymous Ethics Hotline (link in the HR portal)
