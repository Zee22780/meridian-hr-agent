# Code of Conduct

**Last Updated:** January 2026  
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, and interns

---

## Overview

Meridian is committed to maintaining a workplace where every person is treated with dignity and respect. This Code of Conduct sets the standard for professional behavior across all work settings — in-office, remote, and at company events.

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

---

## Core Principles

All employees are expected to:

- Act with honesty and integrity in all professional interactions
- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect regardless of role or seniority
- Take responsibility for their work and their impact on others
- Raise concerns through appropriate channels rather than ignoring them
- Protect confidential company and employee information

---

## Respect and Inclusion

Meridian prohibits discrimination, harassment, and retaliation of any kind. This includes behavior based on:

- Race, color, national origin, or ethnicity
- Gender, gender identity, or gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age
- Religion or belief
- Disability or medical condition
- Pregnancy or parental status
- Veteran status
- Any other characteristic protected by applicable law

**Harassment** includes unwelcome conduct — verbal, written, physical, or visual — that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment. A single severe incident may constitute harassment; it does not need to be repeated.

**Discrimination** includes treating an employee or candidate differently in hiring, compensation, promotion, assignment, or any other employment decision based on a protected characteristic.

---

## Workplace Behavior

### Professional Communication

Employees are expected to communicate professionally in all work contexts — including Slack, email, video calls, and in-person interactions. This includes:

- Avoiding language that is offensive, demeaning, or exclusionary
- Disagreeing respectfully and constructively
- Not making comments about a colleague's appearance, personal life, or identity

### Conflict Resolution

Interpersonal conflicts should be addressed directly when possible. If direct resolution is not possible or appropriate, employees may escalate to their manager or People Operations.

People Operations can facilitate mediation. Employees are encouraged to raise concerns early — unresolved conflict tends to escalate.

### Alcohol and Substances

Meridian does not permit employees to work while impaired. Alcohol may be served at company-sponsored events; employees are expected to drink responsibly and are never required to drink. Illegal substance use on company premises or during work hours is prohibited.

---

## Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict — or appear to conflict — with Meridian's interests. Examples include:

- Working for a direct competitor while employed at Meridian
- Making business decisions that benefit a family member or personal relationship
- Accepting gifts from vendors or clients valued above **$100** without disclosing to your manager
- Starting a business that competes with Meridian's products or services

If you are unsure whether a situation constitutes a conflict of interest, disclose it to your manager or People Operations before proceeding. Undisclosed conflicts discovered after the fact are treated more seriously than conflicts that were proactively disclosed.

---

## Confidentiality

Employees have access to sensitive information about Meridian's business, customers, and colleagues. This information must not be shared outside the company or used for personal gain. Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends.

Sensitive information includes (but is not limited to):

- Unreleased product roadmaps and financial data
- Customer lists, contracts, and pricing
- Employee compensation, performance, and personal data
- Internal communications not intended for public disclosure

---

## Use of Company Resources

Company equipment, software, and systems are provided for business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work or create security risks. Employees may not use company resources to:

- Run a personal business
- Access, store, or distribute inappropriate content
- Circumvent security controls or access systems they are not authorized to use

---

## Reporting a Concern

Employees who witness or experience a violation of this policy are encouraged to report it. Reports can be made to:

- **Your manager** (if they are not involved in the incident)
- **People Operations** at people@meridian.com
- **Anonymous reporting** via the Meridian Ethics Hotline (link available on the HR portal)

All reports are taken seriously and investigated promptly. Retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report is strictly prohibited and is itself a violation of this policy.

---

## Disciplinary Process

Violations are handled on a case-by-case basis. Depending on severity, the response may include:

1. **Verbal warning** — documented in the employee's file
2. **Written warning** — formal documentation with corrective action plan
3. **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** — structured plan with clear expectations and timeline
4. **Suspension** — paid or unpaid, pending investigation
5. **Termination** — immediate in cases of serious violations

Serious violations — including physical violence, sexual harassment, discrimination, or theft — may result in immediate termination without prior warning steps.

Employees subject to disciplinary action have the right to respond to allegations before a final decision is made, except where immediate action is required to protect safety.
