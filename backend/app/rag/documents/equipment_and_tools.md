# Equipment & Tools

**Last Updated:** January 2026  
**Applies To:** All full-time and part-time employees

---

## Overview

Meridian provides the hardware and software employees need to do their jobs. This policy covers what is provided, how to request equipment, your responsibilities as a user, and what happens to equipment when you leave.

---

## Standard Equipment Provisioning

All full-time employees receive a standard equipment package on or before their start date. Equipment is shipped to your address on file for remote employees or available for pickup at your office location for on-site and hybrid employees.

### Standard Package by Role

| Role Category | Laptop | Monitor | Peripherals |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engineering | MacBook Pro 14" (M-series) | 27" external monitor | Keyboard, mouse, USB-C hub |
| Design | MacBook Pro 14" (M-series) | 27" external monitor | Keyboard, mouse, USB-C hub |
| Marketing / Finance / People Ops | MacBook Air 13" (M-series) | Upon request | Keyboard, mouse |
| IT Support | MacBook Pro 14" + Windows PC | Dual monitor setup | Full peripherals |
| Executive | MacBook Pro 16" (M-series) | 27" external monitor | Full peripherals |

**Part-time employees** receive a laptop only. Additional peripherals may be requested and are subject to manager approval.

If your role requires specialized hardware (e.g., a drawing tablet for design, a dedicated GPU for ML work), submit a request through the IT portal. Non-standard equipment requests require manager and IT approval and may take up to **10 business days** to fulfill.

## Software and Licenses

Meridian maintains a standard software stack available to all employees:

- **Communication:** Slack, Zoom
- **Productivity:** Google Workspace (Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Calendar, Drive)
- **Project Management:** Linear (Engineering), Notion (company-wide)
- **Design:** Figma (Design team; view-only licenses for all others)
- **Code:** GitHub (Engineering), Cursor (Engineering)
- **HR & Finance:** Rippling (HR portal, payroll, benefits)

Access to standard tools is provisioned by IT on your first day. You will receive a welcome email from IT with login instructions.

**Role-specific software** not in the standard stack must be requested through the IT portal. Requests require manager approval and a business justification. Meridian will not purchase personal productivity software, entertainment subscriptions, or tools that duplicate existing licensed software.

## Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

Meridian does not officially support a BYOD policy for primary work devices. Employees are expected to use Meridian-issued equipment for work.

Exceptions may be granted by IT on a case-by-case basis for:
- Temporary use while waiting for equipment to arrive (first week only)
- Contractors who use their own devices under a signed security agreement

Personal devices used for work — even temporarily — must comply with Meridian's security requirements: device encryption enabled, OS up to date, and Meridian MDM profile installed if required by IT.

---

## Data Security Requirements

All employees are responsible for protecting Meridian data on their devices:

- **Full-disk encryption** must be enabled on all devices used for work (FileVault on Mac, BitLocker on Windows)
- **Screen lock** must be set to activate after no more than **5 minutes** of inactivity
- **Software updates** must be applied within **14 days** of release for operating system and security patches
- **VPN** is required when accessing internal Meridian systems from public networks (e.g., coffee shops, airports)
- **Sharing credentials** is prohibited — each employee has their own login for all systems

Employees who lose a device or suspect a security breach must notify IT immediately at **it@meridian.com**. Delayed reporting of a lost device may result in disciplinary action.

---

## Equipment Care and Responsibility

Employees are responsible for the reasonable care of Meridian equipment. Normal wear and tear is expected and covered. Damage due to negligence (e.g., spills, drops, improper storage) may be the employee's financial responsibility:

- **First incident:** IT assesses damage; repair cost may be covered at Meridian's discretion
- **Subsequent incidents or gross negligence:** Employee may be billed for repair or replacement cost, deducted from final pay if applicable

Theft of equipment should be reported to IT and, if applicable, to local law enforcement. A police report may be required before a replacement is issued.

---

## Equipment Return at Separation

All Meridian equipment must be returned within **5 business days** of your last day of employment. IT will provide a prepaid shipping label for remote employees. Equipment not returned within this window may result in the replacement value being deducted from your final paycheck, to the extent permitted by applicable law.

Data on returned devices is wiped upon receipt. Employees should not store personal files on Meridian devices — Meridian is not responsible for personal data lost during device wipe.

---

## Upgrades and Replacements

Equipment is typically refreshed on a **3-year cycle**. If your device becomes unusable before the refresh cycle (hardware failure, not negligence), IT will issue a replacement. Requests for early upgrades based on performance preference rather than hardware failure are generally not approved.
