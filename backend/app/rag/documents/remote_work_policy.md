# Remote Work Policy

**Last Updated:** January 2026  
**Applies To:** All full-time employees

---

## Overview

Meridian supports flexible work arrangements and recognizes that many roles can be performed effectively outside of a central office. This policy outlines eligibility, expectations, and responsibilities for remote and hybrid work.

---

## Work Arrangements

Meridian has three official work arrangements:

- **Fully Remote** — Employee works from home or another approved location 100% of the time. No designated office desk.
- **Hybrid** — Employee splits time between a Meridian office and a remote location. Specific in-office requirements vary by team (typically 2–3 days per week).
- **On-Site** — Employee works from a Meridian office full-time. Required for certain roles (see below).

Your work arrangement is determined at hire and reflected in your offer letter. Changes to your arrangement after hire require manager approval and must be submitted to People Operations.

---

## Role-Based Eligibility

Not all roles are eligible for remote or hybrid arrangements. Eligibility is determined by the nature of the work:

**Fully remote eligible:**
- Software Engineering
- Design
- Marketing
- Finance (individual contributor roles)
- People Operations (select roles)

**Hybrid only (not fully remote):**
- Engineering Management
- Department Heads and Directors
- People Operations (HR Generalist, HR Business Partner)

**On-site required:**
- IT Support
- Executive team
- Any role explicitly designated on-site in the offer letter

If you are unsure whether your role is eligible for a different arrangement, contact your manager or People Operations. Role eligibility determinations are made by department heads and are not subject to individual negotiation without explicit approval.

---

## Remote Work Location Requirements

Employees working remotely must do so from an approved location. Approved locations are:

- **Your primary residence** on file with People Operations
- **A secondary residence** (e.g., vacation home) for up to **30 consecutive days per calendar year**, with prior notice to your manager
- **A co-working space** approved by your manager, with costs covered by the employee unless otherwise agreed

**International remote work is not permitted** without explicit written approval from the VP of People Operations and the legal team. Tax and employment law implications vary significantly by country. Requests should be submitted at least 60 days in advance and are not guaranteed to be approved.

Working from a location not on file without prior notice is a policy violation.

---

## Home Office Requirements

Remote employees are responsible for maintaining a workspace that supports productive, professional work:

- Reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download speed recommended)
- A private space suitable for video calls when required
- Compliance with Meridian's data security requirements (see Equipment & Tools policy)

Meridian provides a **one-time home office stipend of $500** for new remote employees to cover equipment or workspace setup costs. This is paid out after 90 days of employment. Receipts are not required, but the stipend is considered taxable income.

---

## Core Hours and Availability

All employees — remote, hybrid, and on-site — are expected to be available during **core hours: 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM in their local time zone**, Monday through Friday.

Outside of core hours, employees have flexibility to structure their workday as needed, provided they meet their responsibilities and communicate their schedule to their team.

Remote employees are expected to:
- Respond to messages within a reasonable timeframe during their working hours
- Attend scheduled meetings (camera-on is encouraged but not required for all meetings)
- Maintain visibility into their availability via their calendar

---

## Equipment

Meridian provides the following to remote and hybrid employees:

- Laptop (Mac or PC, based on role)
- Standard peripherals (keyboard, mouse, monitor) upon request
- Software licenses for required tools

On-site employees may request peripherals but are not provided a home office stipend.

Equipment remains Meridian property and must be returned upon separation. Damage due to negligence may be deducted from final pay in accordance with applicable law.

---

## Performance and Accountability

Remote work is a privilege, not an entitlement. Managers may revoke or modify a remote arrangement if:

- Performance falls below expectations
- The employee is consistently unavailable during core hours
- The role's responsibilities change in a way that requires on-site presence
- There is a pattern of policy violations

Any change to a remote arrangement initiated by the manager must be documented and communicated with at least **2 weeks notice**, except in cases of serious policy violations.

---

## Hybrid Employees: In-Office Expectations

Hybrid employees are expected to align their in-office days with their team's schedule. In-office requirements are set by the department head and communicated by the manager. Employees may not unilaterally choose which days to come in without manager alignment.

Failure to meet in-office requirements without prior approval is treated as an unexcused absence.

---

## Questions and Exceptions

All exceptions to this policy — including international work requests, arrangement changes, or co-working space approvals — must be routed through People Operations. Contact **people@meridian.com** or speak with your HR rep.
